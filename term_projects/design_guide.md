# Term Project Design Guide

Guidelines for designing term project problems, originally used for
weekly Final Challenges and preserved here for future midterm/final projects.

## Design Principles

- **Goal**: test whether the student grasps the week's (or part's) core concept, not
  every topic covered. The problem should revolve around the most important
  idea of the relevant material.
- **Design freedom**: give students room to make their own choices —
  which method to use, which parameters to tune, etc. Encourage them to
  check results visually and iterate. Avoid prescribing a single solution path.
- **Input**: the instructor may provide a specific problem/image as input.
  If not, propose one during the plan phase.
- **Difficulty**: harder than section exercises, but still completable.
  Students already have all the building blocks from the notebooks.
- **Not mandatory for every topic**: if the topics are too elementary or don't lend
  themselves to a meaningful problem, skip it. Quality over quantity.
